Experience

Milestones that shaped me

2012 - 2018

School Experience

My educational journey began in 2007 A.D. (2064 B.S.), but that same year my life changed forever after a tragic attack on our home took my father away. After that painful interruption, I continued my studies and slowly rebuilt confidence through discipline, academics, and resilience.

In 2008 A.D. I resumed school at Our Motherland Academy English Boarding School in Chandrauta, where I studied nursery, LKG, UKG, and Grade 1. I adapted quickly and performed strongly. Later, because of financial difficulty, I moved to Shree Rastriya Secondary School, where I studied from Grade 2 to 5 and consistently remained one of the top students.

Since that school did not continue English-medium education beyond Grade 5, I joined Nepal Adarsh Secondary School for Grade 6 onward. At first I lacked confidence because many students came from private schools, but I still ranked near the top and kept improving. Grade 6 was also the first time I used a computer, and that moment sparked my deep interest in technology.

Grades 7 to 10 became one of the most memorable phases of my life. I built close friendships, enjoyed school tours, sports, and competitions, and became known for solving the Rubik's Cube. I even taught many classmates how to solve it. I also performed well academically, scored 3.73 GPA in the BLE, and continued growing despite the disruption caused by COVID-19.

My school journey was filled with hardship, transformation, friendship, and learning. It shaped both my character and my long-term direction.

2019 - 2021

High School Experience

I chose Computer Science in high school because of my growing love for computers and technology. At first, Grade 11 felt lonely because my old friends were no longer around, and I focused mostly on study rather than social life.

Over time I started making new friends and became more comfortable in my environment. I especially enjoyed practical classes in Physics, Chemistry, and Computer Science, and I loved both the theory and practical side of computing.

I completed Grade 11 with a 3.36 GPA and secured first rank in my class. In Grade 12, I became more connected with juniors and classmates, and many students came to me for help with their studies. I never refused when I could help.

I completed Grade 12 with a 3.26 GPA and again ranked first in my class. What started as a lonely experience became a meaningful and enjoyable chapter by the end.

2021 - 2024

Cybersecurity Enthusiast

My interest in cybersecurity began early through curiosity about computers, mobile devices, and how digital systems work. Later, watching Mr. Robot made that curiosity much stronger and introduced me to the world of hacking and digital security.

In school I studied programming and web technologies like C, HTML, CSS, PHP, JavaScript, and DBMS. I often explored beyond the syllabus and enjoyed building websites on my own. That helped me develop both technical confidence and a sharper problem-solving mindset.

After high school I started learning Python through YouTube and created small projects like a password strength checker and a simple calculator. These projects strengthened my interest in practical coding and security-focused thinking.

My long-term goal is to become a cybersecurity analyst and eventually establish a cyber bureau in Nepal that can help protect digital infrastructure and raise awareness about cyber threats.

2025

US Interview Experience

I had my US visa interview at the US Embassy in Kathmandu. I arrived early, stayed calm, and answered the officer's questions with sincerity and confidence, especially about my interest in Computer Science and cybersecurity.

During the interview, I explained my academic background, my interest in cybersecurity, and how that direction became meaningful to me. I also answered questions about my sponsor and future goals as honestly as I could.

Even though I was prepared, my visa was denied. Walking out with that result was painful, especially after putting so much hope into the process. Still, that experience taught me resilience and reminded me that rejection is not the end of a dream.

For me, that moment became a lesson in patience, faith, and the importance of continuing forward no matter how difficult the setback feels.

About Me

Personal story, grounded in resilience

Hello, I’m Rishi, a passionate and driven Computer Science student with a deep love for learning, building, and transforming hardship into strength. I was born in the peaceful hills of Jukena, Arghakhanchi, Nepal. When I was just three years old, my family moved to Chandrauta in search of better opportunities and a more secure future.

However, life soon tested us in unimaginable ways. In Bhadra 2064 B.S., during the festival of Teej, my family faced a devastating tragedy. While I, my mother, and my sister were at our maternal grandparents’ house, our home in Chandrauta was attacked and burned. In that cruel act, my father was burned alive, and our home and belongings were reduced to ashes. We lost not only shelter, but also the heart of our family.

After that, life became a battle of survival. My mother, with no husband and very limited resources, found the strength to rise and continue. She worked tirelessly to give me and my sister a life filled with hope, dignity, and education. Her strength became the foundation of my character, and her sacrifice became the fuel behind every step I take.

Today, I see technology not just as a career path, but as a tool to create change. I enjoy building websites with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, and I continue exploring programming, design, and cybersecurity with real passion. I believe that every project I make reflects not only technical learning, but the life experience that shaped me.

My background has taught me to value effort, honesty, and clarity. I have learned to build from scratch not only in code, but in life. Each challenge, each lesson, and each success is part of a journey from loss to purpose. My dream is to become a skilled full-stack developer, grow deeply in cybersecurity, and build meaningful technology that helps people.

More than anything, I want to build a life of dignity and success that honors my mother's struggle and gives back to the person who gave me everything. Going beyond limits is not just a phrase for me. It is the way I choose to live.
